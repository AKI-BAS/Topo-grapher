How to import site terrain into SketchUp from real survey data (and win back your time!)
23 June 2026 · 2 min · Aki

Importing site terrain into SketchUp from real survey data might be one of the most important things an architect can learn, whether you’re still a student or ten years into the job. Most of you will disagree with that, which I completely understand. And to be fair, the disagreement is reasonable, because it isn’t the importing itself that matters, and it isn’t your SketchUp skills either. Software can be learned. Talent and an eye for things cannot. Those get carved out slowly, through experience, and curated carefully over years.

So why call importing terrain an important skill at all? Because the real skill is knowing where to spend your time. It’s using your hours on design, where they actually count, instead of bleeding them into modelling and redrawing terrain by hand. Pulling real survey-grade data into your model isn’t a skill in itself. It’s a way to get your time back, so you’re not losing hours (sometimes days) building a terrain that already exists as data. Use your talent where it matters. Don’t waste it tracing contours.

Get your terrain from Topo-grapher instead, because I know that pain firsthand, and that’s exactly why I built the tool.

My name is Aki, and I’m an Architect too. I could not stand to lose any more of my time drawing terrain that should have come from real data instead of guesswork. So I made something that gets you real terrain in seconds, ready to drop into your software. All you have to do is check it against your survey documents, fix whatever needs fixing, and move on. No starting from point zero.
